## Decision 1: Generic codes only (no personal names)

### Rationale

BATB §1.5 Limitation #2: "All candidates, events, organizations, and narratives presented in the game are fictional and are not intended to represent real individuals, political parties, or institutions."

Most defensibly satisfied by never naming anyone at all. Even fictional names like Aurelia/Bruno/Celia can:
- Coincidentally match a real person's name and create defamation exposure (Garbe & Frischlich 2023 *PLoS ONE*).
- Bias student judgments via name-based stereotyping (Crawford & Brandt 2019 *PSPB*).
- Drift over time as new political figures emerge with similar names.

Using only "Candidate A / B / C" matches the literature standard for political-vignette experiments.

### Implementation

- **`scripts/candidate_config.py`** — codes-only mode. `display_name="Candidate A"` (etc.). No `first_name`, `last_name`, `nickname`, or aliases containing personal names. Sanity check raises if anyone tries to set `display_name` to anything else.
- **All 18 templates in `scripts/30_template_scenario_generator.py`** — emit only `Candidate A/B/C`. Verified end-to-end.

### Citations

- Garbe & Frischlich (2023). *PLoS ONE, 18*(4). — candidate-name effects.
- Crawford & Brandt (2019). *PSPB.* — name-based group inference biases.
- Roozenbeek & van der Linden (2019). *Palgrave Communications, 5*(1). — fictional examples in inoculation games.

## Decision 2: Neuro-symbolic GPT-2 conditioning (NOT LoRA)

### What the paper actually claims

BATB §2.6.3 frames M.I.N.E.R.V.A. as a neuro-symbolic pipeline where the graph model captured relational signals, the rule engine translated evidence into "because" statements, and QLattice produced compact expressions approximating credibility decisions.

§1.4 Specific Objective 2 reinforces:

> "To apply symbolic regression (QLattice) to derive interpretable mathematical rules and to extract recurring misinformation patterns that can be used for explanations, unit testing, and **rule-constrained content generation**."

The phrase "rule-constrained content generation" is the architectural commitment: QLattice-derived rules should INFORM content generation. The detector ensemble and DE-GNN provide additional signals the generator should be conditioned on.

### What the v2.5 pipeline actually did

`scripts/10_prepare_gpt2MINERVA.py` registered only TWO kinds of control tokens:
- `<|label=real|>` / `<|label=fake|>`
- `<|graph=high|>` / `<|graph=mid|>` / `<|graph=low|>` / `<|graph=unk|>`

The QLattice equation was used post-hoc to score generations (script 13) but never to shape generation. The detector ensemble was an accept/reject gate (script 12) but not a conditioning signal. The paper's neuro-symbolic claim was partially implemented.

### What v2.6.final does

Three new scripts complete the neuro-symbolic loop:

#### `scripts/10b_prepare_gpt2_neurosymbolic.py` (NEW)

Replaces the v2.5 corpus builder. Joins:
- **Detector probabilities** (`p_roberta_fake`, `p_distil_fake`) from features.
- **DE-GNN confidence** (`p_degnn_fake`) from `data/features/degnn_preds.csv`.
- **QLattice equation** from `models/qlattice_equation.txt` evaluated row-by-row.

Bins each signal and emits training corpus lines with FIVE conditioning tokens:

```
<|label=fake|> <|graph=high|> <|qlat=high|> <|ensem=high|> <|tier=novice|> Pinagtibay ng kongresista...
```

The five tokens:
- `<|label=...|>` (2 values) — class
- `<|graph=...|>` (4 values) — DE-GNN graph confidence in true label
- `<|qlat=...|>` (4 values) — QLattice symbolic-equation confidence
- `<|ensem=...|>` (4 values) — Detector ensemble confidence
- `<|tier=...|>` (4 values) — Teaching difficulty (computed from QLattice margin)

Total: 18 special tokens. **This is the paper's architecture realized in code.**

#### `scripts/11b_train_gpt2_neurosymbolic.py` (NEW)

Loads `jcblaise/gpt2-tagalog`, registers all 18 special tokens, resizes embedding matrix from V_base to V_base+18, fine-tunes 3 epochs at LR 5e-5.

**Preservation of pretrained status (your "retain pretrained" ask):**
First V_base rows of embedding matrix remain byte-identical to the pretrained checkpoint. Only the 18 new rows (~14 KB) are randomly initialized. Other transformer weights are gently fine-tuned at low LR. Base linguistic competence is preserved — model learns to USE the new control tokens to steer existing generation capability rather than relearning Tagalog.

This achieves the same "preserve pretrained, add task-specific machinery" goal as LoRA, but via the mechanism the paper specifies (control tokens) — not a generic technique with no link to M.I.N.E.R.V.A.'s research contribution.

#### `scripts/12b_generate_gpt2_neurosymbolic.py` (NEW)

Inference companion. Prepends all five matching tokens at generation:

```bash
python scripts/12b_generate_gpt2_neurosymbolic.py \
    --target fake --n 500 \
    --graph high --qlat high --ensem high --tier novice \
    --out generated/gpt2_neuro_fake.jsonl
```

Steers GPT-2 toward the joint distribution P(text | label=fake, graph=high, qlat=high, ensem=high, tier=novice) — i.e. the slice of training examples that were correctly labelled, confidently classified by graph + symbolic + ensemble, and clear enough to be teaching cards.

### Defense narrative

> "M.I.N.E.R.V.A. operationalizes a neuro-symbolic pipeline. The detectors (RoBERTa, DistilBERT) extract dense linguistic signals; PCA reduces them to a tabular feature space; the DE-GNN aggregates relational structure into a graph-confidence score; the QLattice symbolic regression produces an interpretable equation. v2.6.final then **uses all four signals as control tokens** for GPT-2 generation. By conditioning on these signals — not just labels — the generator preferentially produces text that matches the distribution of cleanly-classifiable training examples. This is the rule-constrained content generation the paper specifies in Objective 2."

## What v2.6.final does NOT change

- **Detector model weights** (RoBERTa, DistilBERT, DE-GNN) are not retrained.
- **Template content** is unchanged (18 templates from v2.6-final-consolidated remain as-is).
- **Pool composition logic** (scripts 21, 23, 24, 28) is unchanged.
- **Pipeline entry points** unchanged. New scripts (10b/11b/12b/33/34) are additive — they don't replace existing scripts (10/11/12).
