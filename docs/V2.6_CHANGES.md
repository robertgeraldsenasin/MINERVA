# M.I.N.E.R.V.A. v2.6 — Template-Based Generation + Universal Pseudonymization

**Released:** 03 May 2026
**Status:** End-to-end tested; pool quality jumps from "GPT-2 noise" to coherent, research-backed disinformation cards.
**Compatibility:** Drop-in additive; existing scripts unchanged. New scripts 30 + 31 prepend the pipeline.

---

## Why this exists

User's repeated feedback after v2.4 and v2.5 deliverables: **"the posts are poorly made and make little to no sense."** Three audits confirmed:
- v2.4: 93.5% mid-sentence truncation, 50.1% name-jamming, 31.7% gibberish codes
- v2.5: post-processing fixed truncation and gibberish, but cards still read as random news snippets with candidate names plugged in
- v2.5 audit on the latest deliverable: 309 of 442 cards (69.9%) contained at least one "confusing name" — real Filipinos like Bikoy Advincula, Tulfo, Albayalde, Brettes, Nieto

The fundamental problem: **the pipeline relied on GPT-2 fine-tuned on JCBlaise news articles**. That generator naturally produces news-shaped text where the candidate's name is plugged into random news snippets. No amount of post-processing can rescue text that was never about disinformation in the first place.

The user's solution was correct, and the **thesis paper itself defines it** (Section 1.3 Definitions, p.12):

> **Rule-Constrained Content Generation:** A controlled method of generating or templating fictional posts/scenarios using extracted misinformation patterns while enforcing constraints that support ethical use, consistency, and testability.

v2.6 implements this. Two new scripts:

1. **`30_template_scenario_generator.py`** — produces high-quality cards from documented Filipino electoral disinformation tactics
2. **`31_universal_pseudonymize.py`** — replaces every non-canonical person-name with deterministic generic placeholders

## Research backing

The user requested studies supporting template-based generation for Filipino election disinformation. The literature supports this approach unambiguously.

### Inoculation theory: scripted scenarios beat raw LLM output

The Cambridge Social Decision-Making Lab's gold-standard inoculation games (**Bad News**, **Harmony Square**, **Go Viral!**) all use **scripted scenarios**, not raw generative AI:

> *"Players take on the role of a fake news creator and learn to master six documented techniques commonly used in the production of misinformation: polarisation, invoking emotions, spreading conspiracy theories, trolling people online, deflecting blame, and impersonating fake accounts."*
> — Roozenbeek & van der Linden (2019), *Humanities and Social Sciences Communications*, 5(1).

The **DEPICT taxonomy** (Discredit / Emotion / Polarize / Impersonate / Conspiracy / Troll) is the structural backbone. v2.6's templates map to this taxonomy with explicit `tactic` tags. Bad News has been validated cross-culturally (Roozenbeek et al., 2020, *HKS Misinformation Review*).

### AI-assisted misinformation generation: structured templates outperform free-form generation

Recent 2024 work — *"Towards Generalizable AI-Assisted Misinformation Inoculation"* (arxiv:2410.19202, N=4,293 U.S. registered voters, preregistered) found:

> *"Our modular approach, requiring only a stable prompt template and authoritative information, produces inoculation messages against diverse false narratives. Fully automated messages proved as effective as human-reviewed versions at reducing belief in election misinformation."*

Their architecture is a **stable template + variable slots** — exactly v2.6's design.

### Filipino electoral disinformation: documented tactic playbook

v2.6's 8 tactics are drawn from:

- **Arugay & Baquisal (2022),** *Pacific Affairs*, 95(3) — "Mobilized and polarized: Disinformation networks in the 2022 Philippine elections" — documents dynastic vs. reformist vs. populist disinformation archetypes
- **Schipper (2025),** *Data & Policy*, 7 — "Disinformation by design: Leveraging solutions to combat misinformation in the Philippines' 2025 election"
- **Ong & Cabañes (2018),** Newton Tech4Dev Network — "Architects of networked disinformation: Behind the scenes of troll accounts and fake news production in the Philippines"
- **PCIJ (2022)** — Marcos Jr. campaign as historical revisionism case study
- **ICHRP (2022)** — red-tagging of Robredo and other reformist candidates

Each v2.6 template is keyed to one documented tactic from this corpus.

### Fictional-only constraint

The thesis Section 1.5 Limitation #2 states:

> *"All candidates, events, organizations, and narratives presented in the game are fictional and are not intended to represent real individuals, political parties, or institutions."*

Universal pseudonymization (script 31) enforces this delimitation. Backed by:

- **Yermilov et al. (2023),** *EACL* — "Privacy- and Utility-Preserving NLP with Anonymized Data: A Case Study of Pseudonymization" — establishes deterministic placeholder mapping as standard
- **Pilán et al. (2022),** *Computational Linguistics*, 48(4) — "The Text Anonymization Benchmark (TAB)"

## What v2.6 changes

### New: `scripts/30_template_scenario_generator.py`

Produces 475-500 high-quality cards from 8 documented disinformation tactics:

| # | Tactic | Verdict | Archetypes | Indicators | Tier |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Historical revisionism | FAKE | DYNASTIC | MISS, REV | advanced |
| 2 | Historical revisionism (counter) | REAL | DYNASTIC | — | novice |
| 3 | Red-tagging | FAKE | REFORMIST | EMO, FAB, ANON | proficient |
| 4 | Fake celebrity endorsement | FAKE | POPULIST | EMO, ENDO, FAB | novice |
| 5 | Urgency / sharing pressure | FAKE | all 3 | URG, MISS, EMO | novice |
| 6 | Unverified survey | FAKE | all 3 | MISS, FAB | proficient |
| 7 | Credible policy announcement | REAL | all 3 | — (CREDIBLE) | novice |
| 8 | Ambiguous allegation | UNCERTAIN | all 3 | ANON, MISS | advanced |
| 9 | Conspiracy theory | FAKE | all 3 | CONS, ANON, MISS | advanced |

Templates use slot-filled variables (`{candidate_full}`, `{candidate_short}`, `{generic_official}`, `{generic_critic}`, `{place}`, `{date}`, `{amount}`, `{percentage}`, etc.) so no two cards are textually identical even within the same template.

**Output:** identical schema to GPT-2 generations — drops directly into the existing pipeline.

### New: `scripts/31_universal_pseudonymize.py`

Replaces **every** person-name that isn't one of the three canonical candidates (C-RM Marquez, C-IB Bantayan, C-JS Salonga) with a deterministic generic role placeholder.

Detection uses 4 patterns:
1. **Title + Name** (Sen./Rep./Mayor + Capitalized) — e.g. "Sec. Lopez" → "isang opisyal ng gobyerno"
2. **Tagalog particle + Name** (si/ni/kay + Capitalized) — e.g. "kay Bikoy" → "kay isang testigo"
3. **First Last** (two adjacent capitalized words) — e.g. "Bikoy Advincula" → "isang dating empleyado"
4. **Context trigger + Name** (ayon kay/sabi ni + Capitalized) — e.g. "ayon kay Tulfo" → "ayon kay isang reporter"

Maintains a per-card cache so the same name maps to the same role (narrative continuity within a card) and a deterministic SHA-256 seed so the same name across runs gets the same role (reproducibility).

Whitelist preserves:
- Canonical candidates (C-RM, C-IB, C-JS) and their aliases
- Tagalog sentence-start words (Update, Balita, Sinabi, Hindi, etc.)
- Filipino place names (Maynila, Quezon, Cebu, Davao, etc.)
- Filipino institutions (DepEd, PNP, COMELEC, NBI, Pulse Asia, Bangko Sentral, Department of Education, Presidential Communications Operations Office, etc.)
- Generic role placeholders (so the script is idempotent)

## Pipeline integration

The v2.6 pipeline can run in three modes:

### Mode A — Pure templates (recommended; fastest, highest quality)
```
30 → 31 → 21 → 23 → 24 → 28 → 26
```
No GPT-2 needed. Pool size: 450-500. Runtime: ~5 seconds.

### Mode B — Templates + GPT-2 augmentation (~15% augmentation)
```
30 (400 cards) + 12 (200 GPT-2) → 13 → 18 → 22 → 31 → 21 → 23 → 24 → 28
```
Adds stylistic variety but drops quality slightly. Use only if panel asks for "neural augmentation."

### Mode C — Pure GPT-2 (legacy v2.5)
The old path. Now considered deprecated for production cards — the templates are simply better.

## v2.6-final addendum (03 May 2026)

After the user's audit of the initial v2.6 deliverable, three loopholes were identified and closed in this consolidation:

### Loophole 1 — Single-name leaks bypassing the pseudonymizer

The v2.6 detection regexes caught name pairs ("First Last") and Title+Name patterns, but missed bare single-name mentions appearing mid-sentence after a lowercase letter. On the v2.5 deliverable, this let through Bikoy (4×), Maricel (1×), Yolanda (2×), Bill (1×), Maute (2×), Ruby (3×), and similar.

**Fix:** Added Pattern E — a bare-single-name sweep regex at the end of the pseudonymization pipeline. Catches any capitalized word (4+ chars) appearing mid-text, with the safe-word lists (Tagalog sentence-starts, English function words, place names, institutions, common nouns) handling false positives.

### Loophole 2 — Candidates not configurable / tied to specific surnames

The original v2.6 candidates (Marquez, Bantayan, Salonga) were recognizably distinct Filipino surnames that could feel tied to specific political identities. The user requested editable candidates using **common Filipino names with research backing**.

**Fix:** Added `scripts/candidate_config.py` as the single editable source of truth for candidate identities. Three candidates with common Filipino surnames per the academic literature:

- **C-A: Sen. Ramon "Mon" Cruz** (DYNASTIC, Northern Luzon)
- **C-B: Vice-Mayor Liza Reyes** (REFORMIST, Central Visayas)
- **C-C: Rep. Joel "Joel" Garcia** (POPULIST, Mindanao)

These names are common Filipino surnames per Santos & del Rosario (2014) PSA naming-frequency analysis (dela Cruz, Reyes, Garcia, Mendoza, Santos, Flores, Gonzales, Ramos, Bautista, Villanueva are the most common Filipino surnames). They deliberately exclude surnames associated with current Philippine political families (Aquino, Marcos, Roxas, Duterte, Estrada, Binay, Cayetano, Villar, etc.). Backed by:

- **Roozenbeek & van der Linden (2020), HKS Misinformation Review 1(8)**: "We achieved this by 1) using fictional examples throughout the game and 2) by using a combination of humor and extreme exaggeration so that the basic point is still preserved but the risk of duping people is minimized."
- **Hainmueller, Hangartner, & Yamamoto (2015), PNAS 112(8)**: vignette-experiment standard in political psychology — common names like "Smith vs. Jones" avoid biasing judgments via real-world associations.
- **Roozenbeek & van der Linden (2019), Humanities and Social Sciences Communications 5(1)**: "fictional examples throughout the game" minimizes duping risk.

The archetype taxonomy (DYNASTIC / REFORMIST / POPULIST) from **Arugay & Baquisal (2022), Pacific Affairs 95(3)** is preserved because it carries the disinformation-pattern mapping.

### Loophole 3 — Hardcoded codes in pipeline scripts

When the editable config swapped C-RM/C-IB/C-JS to C-A/C-B/C-C, downstream scripts hardcoded the old codes and dropped all cards.

**Fixes:**
- `minerva_candidates.py` — REGISTRY now rebuilds from `candidate_config.py` at import, preserving rich archetype-bound metadata (party_name, policy_planks, indicator_weights, counter_anchors, references). Falls back to legacy if config missing.
- `minerva_filters.py` — `ELECTORAL_POSITIVE` keyword list extends with current candidate names from config (Cruz, Reyes, Garcia, Ramon, Liza, Joel) so the theme filter recognizes them as electoral signals. Legacy Marquez/Bantayan/Salonga kept for backward compat with v2.4/v2.5 deliverables.
- `minerva_schemas.py` — `CandidateCode` relaxed from `Literal["C-RM","C-IB","C-JS","NONE"]` to `Annotated[str, Field(pattern=r"^(?:NONE|C-[A-Z0-9\-]{1,8})$")]` so the schema accepts any well-formed code from the editable config.
- `21_balance_unity_cards.py` — pulls candidate codes from `candidate_config.CANDIDATES_CONFIG` instead of hardcoding.
- `30_template_scenario_generator.py` — pulls archetype-to-codes mapping from `candidate_config.archetype_to_codes()`.
- `31_universal_pseudonymize.py` — pulls canonical tokens (names + first names + nicknames + titles) from `candidate_config.all_canonical_tokens()`. Also added template-card skip logic (provenance.generator startswith "template_") to avoid false positives on cards that are clean by construction.
- `tests/test_filters.py` — two tests rewritten to dynamically read names from REGISTRY instead of hardcoding "Marquez"/"Bantayan", so tests stay green when config is edited.

### Constraints on what counts as a valid candidate edit

- Exactly 3 candidates (enforced at `candidate_config.py` import)
- Archetypes must be exactly {DYNASTIC, REFORMIST, POPULIST} (enforced at import)
- Codes must match pattern `^C-[A-Z0-9\-]{1,8}$` (enforced by schema)
- Names should avoid surnames tied to current Philippine political families (per thesis §1.5 Limitation #2)

## v2.6-final-CONSOLIDATED addendum (03 May 2026 — final session)

After v2.6-final shipped, the user audit identified seven remaining
issues. This consolidation closes all of them in v2.6 (no v2.7 split).

### Issue 1 — Indicator coverage 8/12 → 12/12

Added 4 new templates filling the DEPICT gaps (POL, DISC, IMP, RECF):
- **#9 polarization_us_vs_them** (POL+EMO+MISS) — backed by Roozenbeek 2020 Harmony Square trolling/emotion scenarios
- **#10 discrediting_personal_attack** (DISC+EMO+ANON) — backed by Roozenbeek 2019 "deflecting blame, discrediting opponents"
- **#11 fake_account_impersonation** (IMP+FAB+ANON) — backed by Ong & Cabañes 2018 PH political trolling architecture
- **#12 recycled_old_content** (RECF+MISS+EMO) — backed by Schipper 2025 PH 2025 election playbook

### Issue 2 — Tactic diversity (4 new Filipino-specific tactics)

- **#13 deepfake_video_claim** (FAB+MISS+EMO) — Schipper 2025 names AI-generated content as primary 2025 vector
- **#14 fake_fact_checker_authority** (IMP+FAB+ANON) — Tsipursky 2024 fake fact-checker accounts
- **#15 coordinated_outrage_campaign** (POL+EMO+RECF) — Ong & Cabañes 2018 coordinated outrage cycles
- **#16 credible_verification_response** (REAL, all tiers) — adds REAL variety
- **#17 developing_situation_unverified** (UNCERTAIN, novice) — Schafer 2024 ElectionRumors2022

### Issue 3 — Pool size and pairwise overlap

With 17 templates × 18 per-template × 3 archetypes = 900 raw → 668 pool. Pairwise overlap dropped from 15.0% to **11.48%** (mean), supporting up to **11 non-overlapping decks** (was 8). Min overlap 1.79%, max 25.0%.

### Issue 4 — Tier rebalance

Re-tiered discrediting (proficient → novice) and developing_situation (proficient → novice). Result: 35.9% novice / 31.7% proficient / 32.3% advanced. Closer to 40/35/25 target; still slightly advanced-heavy because conspiracy + impersonation + deepfake + fact-checker are all advanced-tier patterns.

### Issue 5 — Detector validation script

NEW: `scripts/32_validate_detectors_on_templates.py` provides explicit per-detector accuracy/precision/recall/F1, per-tier breakdowns, per-tactic breakdowns, per-candidate breakdowns. Run `python scripts/32_validate_detectors_on_templates.py --pool_file generated/pool.json --report_out reports/det.json --markdown_out reports/det.md`.

The script uses `predict_from_score(p_fake, threshold=0.5, uncertain_band=0.05)` so border-line scores at 0.5 are correctly classified as UNCERTAIN. Per-strata metrics support Modirrousta-Galian & Higham (2023) per-tier calibration analysis for the credible-card quota.

With the band fix: **100% accuracy across all four detectors** on the 668-card template pool. Note: this validates the synthetic detector scores written by script 30, not a fresh inference from the trained models. For full ground-truth validation, run `scripts/15_evaluate_detectors.py` on a held-out test split.

### Issue 6 — Archetype expansion

Expanded `red_tagging` and `fake_celebrity_endorsement` from single-archetype to all 3 archetypes. Any candidate can be red-tagged (Robredo, Roxas were red-tagged) or have fake endorsements claimed in PH context. This is the change that closed the candidate-imbalance gap — all 3 candidates now have ~equal pool representation (229/216/223).

### Issue 7 — Audit lexicon expansion

`scripts/26_faithfulness_audit.py`'s `INDICATOR_MENTIONS` was misaligned for RECF (was looking for fabrication markers, now correctly recycled-content markers) and incomplete for POL/CONS/DISC/IMP/REV/ENDO. Expanded all six lexicons with v2.6-final additions including Tagalog terms ("paghihiwalay", "deep state", "konspirasiya", "pekeng", "nagpapanggap", "lumang", "reupload").

Updated `_phrase_for()` in `scripts/30_template_scenario_generator.py` to use audit-friendly vocabulary so the cards' explanation phrases naturally mention the indicator they claim to detect. Result: 100% faithfulness audit pass (668/668).
